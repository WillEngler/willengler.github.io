The software industry is full of systems like Agile, Lean, and Extreme Programming. If you ask a software team how they manage projects and they say, "Agile with two week sprints," you instantly have a decent idea of how they operate.

If someone asked me how I manage my studies, I could not give such a clean answer. Instead of following a coherent process, I'm in the habit of blindly following syllabi. I dutifully complete problem sets, take notes in lecture, read the required texts, and not much else.

<b>I can do better.</b>

I want to reclaim my learning in the same way that [the agile movement][agile] reclaimed software from waterfall development. Because all good things have an acronym, I will name my learning methodology BDL: Blog Driven Learning. Its tenets are as follows: 

-	Instead of cramming the assigned material, direct my own learning.
-	Instead of memorizing definitions, write code to illustrate new concepts.
-	Instead of taking notes for my consumption, write accessible blog posts.
-	Instead of learning alone, form a community of students.

Over the coming semester, I'll try to put BDL into practice. Will I stick to it? Will it make me a more productive student? Only one way to find out!
